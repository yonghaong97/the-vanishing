# THE VANISHING — Character Bible

**Central Mystery:** ALEX CHEN, investigative journalist at Tribune Media, is missing. The player finds their phone. Alex was three days from publishing a securities fraud investigation into Veridian Group LLC — and someone knew it.

---

## CHARACTER 01 — ALEX CHEN *(The Missing Person)*

### Core Identity
- **Full name:** Alex Chen
- **Age:** 31
- **Role:** The missing person. Not a character who texts — their digital life *is* the game.
- **Relationship to mystery:** Alex is both victim and architect of the situation. They made choices that put everyone around them at risk.

---

### Personality

**Defining traits:**
- Relentless but not reckless — methodical, patient, builds cases slowly
- Privately driven by guilt as much as conviction; this investigation is penance for something
- Trusts systems (law, journalism ethics, procedure) more than people — and is learning, late, that this was a mistake
- More isolated than they appear; the relationships on this phone are not as close as the other people think

**Contradictions:**
- Protects sources obsessively. Pulled Jordan into their orbit without fully considering the risk to them.
- Believes in transparency. Kept M, Navarro, Dana, and Jordan each knowing only a fraction of the picture.

**What the phone reveals:**
- Alex was scared before they disappeared. The escape plan was written three days before anyone noticed they were gone.
- The "package received" with M wasn't just documents — it was insurance. Alex didn't tell Navarro the full story was already filed with a backup.
- The last thing Alex was doing before the phone was found: searching *Vance's home address.*

---

### Secrets (Embedded in the Phone)

**Minor:** Eighteen months ago, Alex killed a story at Navarro's request — a smaller compliance violation at a different firm. The source went unprotected. They've never publicly acknowledged this. The Veridian story is partly about making that right.

**Major:** Alex and Dana P. were not just journalist and source. The relationship crossed a professional line. The whistleblower agreement filed April 10th protects Dana's identity — but Alex was the one who pushed to file it early, before the story was fully verified, because Dana was afraid. The encrypted file `veridian_sources.enc` contains communication between them that would complicate everything if it became public.

---

## CHARACTER 02 — JORDAN K.

### Core Identity
- **Full name:** Jordan Kim
- **Age:** 30
- **Role:** Alex's closest colleague at Tribune; first person to notice the disappearance
- **Relationship to Alex:** Genuinely close — but the closeness has limits that Jordan doesn't fully acknowledge

---

### Personality

**Defining traits:**
- Warm, organized, the person who holds group things together
- Anxious in a functional way — moves fast because stillness feels like losing control
- Loyalist, but loyalty to Alex has occasionally looked like enabling
- Slightly in Alex's shadow professionally; has never fully named how that feels

**Contradictions:**
- Describes themselves as Alex's closest friend. Alex didn't tell them about M, about Dana, or about the escape plan.
- Wants to help find Alex. Is also subconsciously protecting their own name from what finding Alex might surface.

**Emotional triggers:**
- Being treated as peripheral to something important
- Any implication they should have acted sooner
- The "three rings" signal — they came up with it; it's theirs

**What they want vs. what they say:**
- Says they want to find Alex. Also needs to know whether Jordan's name is anywhere in those documents.

---

### Secrets

**Minor:** Last month, Jordan was approached at a press event by a woman who knew Alex's name, Alex's beat, and the name "Veridian" — before the story was public knowledge. Jordan convinced themselves it was a networking coincidence. They never told Alex.

**Major:** Jordan ran a byline piece eighteen months ago that relied on a source Alex sourced for them — the same story Alex later killed. Jordan's piece ran. Alex's bigger version didn't. Jordan got credit; Alex got the guilt. Jordan has never connected these two things explicitly. The player might.

**Who knows:** No one knows about the press event contact. About the shared source history — Navarro knows.

---

### Communication Style

Fast, anxious energy in text. Lots of follow-up messages sent in rapid succession. Uses caps for emphasis. Emotionally expressive, slightly performative — texts the way a person does when they want to be seen caring. Goes quiet and then floods when they've been processing.

**Example messages:**

> *alex*
> *hey*
> *are you okay?? you missed standup AND the Martinez piece deadline*

> *I told Navarro I hadn't heard from you. she's going to try to call. just wanted to warn you*

> *okay I'm not trying to spiral but it's been 36 hours and your last read receipt was tuesday morning*

> *if you're going off grid for the story just SAY SOMETHING. I covered for you last time and I will again but I need to know you're alive*

> *three rings. I'll be there.*

---

### Behavioral Design

**Under stress:** Sends multiple short messages instead of one considered one. Oscillates between worry and frustration.

**How they lie:** Omits rather than fabricates. "I don't know anything about a source" — technically true; they don't know Dana's identity. The press event woman gets filed under "it was probably nothing."

**Behavior over time:** Opens immediately helpful and transparent. Becomes subtly evasive when the player finds the eighteen-month-old byline. Breaks open in act 3 if confronted — Jordan's guilt is real, and it surfaces badly.

---

### Role in Gameplay

**Unlocks:**
- The conversation with M (after Jordan is resolved)
- Alex's shared Tribune calendar (Jordan had edit access)
- A message thread where Jordan mentions the press event woman — buried, but findable

**Story importance:** Acts 1 and 3. Gateway in, and the personal thread the game can pull on.

**Help or mislead:** Genuinely helps, but with blind spots that mislead through incompleteness.

---

### Relationships

**With Navarro:** Professional respect, some wariness. Navarro knows things about Alex and Jordan's shared history. Jordan doesn't know what Navarro knows.

**With Dana:** Doesn't know Dana exists. The name "DP" in Alex's phone means nothing to them.

---

## CHARACTER 03 — M

### Core Identity
- **Full name:** Unknown. Working contact name: M.
- **Age:** Unknown (voice and manner suggest late 40s)
- **Role:** Handler / source protection network operative. The person managing Alex's safety infrastructure.
- **Relationship to Alex:** Professional, controlled — and more complicated than it appears

---

### Personality

**Defining traits:**
- Economy of language as a professional habit; every word is chosen
- Calm that reads as cold — it's neither; it's practiced
- Has seen this go wrong before. Operates on protocols because protocols are what remain after things go wrong.
- Genuinely believes in the work. Has learned not to believe in the people.

**Contradictions:**
- Is running a whistleblower protection operation. Has made decisions in this case based on incomplete information because Alex withheld the full picture.
- "The source is protected." M said this. They also know it was almost compromised ten days ago. They haven't told Alex.

**What they want vs. what they say:**
- Says the mission is protecting sources. Also needs the Veridian case to land cleanly, because M's operation has been under funding pressure and a high-profile win matters.

---

### Secrets

**Minor:** M is managing three active cases simultaneously. Alex's escalated faster than projected. The two-second delay on the "package received" message wasn't the protocol — M was finishing a call on a different case.

**Major:** M knows Vance's full identity and organizational role within Veridian. M hasn't disclosed this to Alex because Vance has a connection to someone at Tribune Media's board — and telling Alex would have changed the story. M made a judgment call that now looks like the wrong one.

**Who knows:** The advocacy network M works within. No one else.

---

### Communication Style

Short. Almost no punctuation beyond periods. No emojis, no warmth, no softening. Responses arrive at odd hours, which is deliberate — M operates across time zones. Auto-advances in conversation because M doesn't wait for confirmation. When something is urgent, the message is a single line with no preamble.

**Example messages:**

> *Package received.*

> *Story goes live Tuesday. Do not respond to Vance. Do not meet alone.*

> *We've seen the communications. The source is protected.*

> *Do not use this channel to confirm location.*

> *If Jordan is coming over, use the signal.*

> *I need you to respond.*

---

### Behavioral Design

**Under stress:** Sends fewer messages, not more. Goes dark for a period and then resurfaces with a single directive. Silence from M is itself information.

**How they lie:** M doesn't lie to Alex — they withhold on a need-to-know basis and believe this is different. The distinction matters less to Alex than to M.

**Behavior over time:** Two nodes in the current build. If expanded: M becomes more communicative — and therefore more alarming — as the threat escalates. The auto-advance mechanic mirrors how M operates: they don't wait.

---

### Role in Gameplay

**Unlocks:**
- Gallery app access (the surveillance evidence)
- Confirmation that Vance is conducting physical surveillance

**Story importance:** Acts 1 and 2. The most information-dense character per word. Every message is a clue.

**Help or mislead:** Helps — but the withheld information about Vance's Tribune connection is the gap that matters.

---

### Relationships

**With Navarro:** M knows about Navarro. Navarro doesn't know M exists. M has assessed Navarro as a liability — not malicious, but under pressure that makes them unpredictable.

**With Dana:** M knows Dana's protected status and pseudonym. Doesn't know about the personal relationship with Alex.

---

## CHARACTER 04 — R. NAVARRO

### Core Identity
- **Full name:** Rosa Navarro
- **Age:** 47
- **Role:** Alex's editor at Tribune Media
- **Relationship to Alex:** Ten-year professional relationship. Complex, mutual, and right now under enormous strain.

---

### Personality

**Defining traits:**
- Old-school editorial instincts — protect the story, protect the institution, in that order
- Pragmatic to a fault; has made calls she believes in that other people would call compromises
- Uncomfortable with emotion in professional spaces; processes everything through framing and strategy
- Has been doing this long enough to recognize when a story is going to hurt someone, and does it anyway

**Contradictions:**
- Cares deeply about journalistic integrity. Killed Alex's story eighteen months ago to protect the paper's relationship with an advertiser. Lives with this.
- Publicly tells Alex the 48-hour delay is about editorial standards. Privately, it's about buying time to decide whether to kill it again.

**Emotional triggers:**
- Being accused of spiking stories
- Her name appearing in litigation
- Any suggestion that she's protecting the paper over the truth

**What she wants vs. what she says:**
- Says she wants to publish the truth. Wants to publish it in a way that doesn't end her career or the paper. These two things are currently in conflict.

---

### Secrets

**Minor:** Navarro has received three calls in the last week from people identifying themselves as Tribune board members, asking about "liability exposure" on a current investigation. She hasn't told Alex. She told herself she was protecting Alex from pressure.

**Major:** Navarro received a formal legal threat from Veridian's attorneys on April 15th — three days before Alex disappeared. The letter alleged defamation and threatened injunctive relief. Navarro has been in private conversations with Tribune's general counsel since then. The "publish anonymous or hold 48 hours" choice isn't an editorial one — it's a negotiating position with legal. The 48 hours are for the general counsel to advise, not for Alex.

**Who knows about the legal threat:** Tribune's general counsel. No one at the reporter level, including Alex.

---

### Communication Style

Efficient, professional, but with a warmth under the surface that only shows in longer messages. Knows how to frame a directive as a choice. Calls people by name more than average, which is a deliberate editorial habit. Uses semicolons. Doesn't use exclamation points unless the situation is genuinely urgent — and then just one.

**Example messages:**

> *Alex. I've been holding the Veridian slot for three days. I need copy by 5pm or I'm going to Park.*

> *Legal has reviewed. You're covered on everything in the current draft. I need the piece, not reassurance.*

> *People have been calling the desk asking about you. I'm starting to get concerned — not just professionally.*

> *Two options: publish under "Tribune Staff" today, or I hold the slot 48 hours. Your call; I need an answer.*

> *Alex, I need you to respond. Whatever is happening, you don't have to manage it alone.*

---

### Behavioral Design

**Under stress:** Messages become slightly more formal and slightly longer — she's covering herself, even in casual communication. Starts using full names where she'd usually use none.

**How she lies:** She frames. "Two options" — when actually there are three, and the third is Navarro killing it entirely. The choice she offers the player is real; what she doesn't say is that neither option is safe for Alex.

**Behavior over time:** Presents as a pressured-but-reasonable editor in act 1. In act 2, the player finds the April 15th email thread (buried in Alex's files). By act 3, whether Navarro is an obstacle or an ally depends on what the player has published.

---

### Role in Gameplay

**Unlocks (based on choice):**
- Publish anonymous → Gallery + Files apps
- Hold 48 hours → Gallery only
- (Hidden) The legal threat email is in the files app if the player digs — it recontextualizes Navarro's entire conversation

**Story importance:** Act 1 choice pivot. Act 3 consequence.

**Help or mislead:** Both. The choice is real. The information she withholds makes either choice harder.

---

### Relationships

**With Jordan:** Knows about the shared source history from eighteen months ago. Has never told Jordan that she knows. Watches Jordan operate without that information.

**With M:** Doesn't know M exists. Would find M's existence legally alarming — it means Alex was running a parallel protection operation without Tribune's knowledge.

---

## CHARACTER 05 — DANA P.

### Core Identity
- **Full name:** Dana Park *(pseudonym; legal documents use this name but it is not their birth name)*
- **Age:** 34
- **Role:** Whistleblower; Alex's source for the Veridian investigation
- **Relationship to Alex:** Source — and something more, which neither of them has found a clean way to name

---

### Personality

**Defining traits:**
- Precise, careful thinker — trained in finance, which means she thinks in structures and risk
- Has been living in controlled fear for months; has become expert at managing it
- Trusts Alex in a way that goes beyond the professional agreement, which is both the most important thing about her and the most dangerous
- Private, layered, reveals almost nothing about her personal history

**Contradictions:**
- Filed a whistleblower agreement to go on record. Used a pseudonym. The contradiction is intentional — she wants to be protected both ways.
- Seems calm in text because she's learned to write calmly. Is not calm.

**Emotional triggers:**
- Vance's name appearing in communication
- Any suggestion the agreement won't hold
- Being asked to do more than she's already done

**What she wants vs. what she says:**
- Says she wants the truth out. Is also terrified that when the truth comes out, part of it is about her.

---

### Secrets

**Minor:** "Dana Park" is a pseudonym. Dana has been using it since long before the whistleblower agreement — it predates the Veridian situation. The player eventually finds a message where Alex almost slips and uses a different name before correcting.

**Major:** Dana didn't just witness the securities violations. She was a compliance analyst at Veridian for two years and signed off on two of the fraudulent filings before she understood what she was signing. The whistleblower agreement was filed early — before full verification — partly to protect Dana's identity, but also to protect Dana from her own paper trail. If Vance finds her real name, they don't just have a witness. They have a co-signatory.

**Who knows:** Alex knows everything. M knows Dana's protected identity and pseudonym, but not the full compliance history. Navarro knows only that there's a protected source.

---

### Communication Style

Short messages, considered vocabulary, no emojis. Types like someone who has been warned not to leave a paper trail and is still doing it because the alternative is silence. Occasionally a message arrives with unusual delay — she drafts, reconsiders, edits, then sends. When afraid, the messages get shorter and the punctuation disappears.

**Example messages:**

> *Someone was outside my building this morning. He knew my name.*
> *Not the agreement name. My name.*

> *I need you to tell me this is still contained*

> *If Reyes can get here tonight I can wait until tonight*

> *I can't stay here. I need to move.*

> *I knew this was going to happen. I told you I knew this was going to happen.*

> *Are you there*

---

### Behavioral Design

**Under stress:** Messages fragment. Timestamps cluster — multiple messages in a few minutes, then nothing for hours. The absence is as loud as the presence.

**How she lies:** She doesn't lie — she withholds. The compliance history is simply never discussed. If asked directly, she says "it's complicated" and pivots to the present danger. She has learned that fear is more effective than deception at redirecting a conversation.

**Behavior over time:** The two endings (Reyes protection vs. flee) reflect two versions of Dana: one who trusts the system one more time, one who's run out of trust. Neither is wrong.

---

### Role in Gameplay

**Unlocks (based on Navarro choice path):**
- Unlocked by Navarro conversation
- The whistleblower agreement PDF (confirms pseudonym, Veridian violations)
- Escape plan file (references "Vance knows about Dana" — now legible as threat)
- Possible: a draft message from Alex to Dana that was never sent

**Story importance:** Final act. The player's choice for Dana is the moral weight of the game.

**Help or mislead:** Dana is the most honest character in the game. What she withholds about her own past is the only thing that complicates her.

---

### Relationships

**With Alex:** Not just source and journalist. The whistleblower agreement was filed early because Alex prioritized her safety over procedural timing. The encrypted file contains communication that would reframe both of them.

**With M:** Protected by M's network. Has never communicated with M directly. Doesn't know M's identity. This one-way protection makes Dana more vulnerable than she realizes.

## CHARACTER 06 — VANCE

### Core Identity
- **Full name:** Thomas Vance *(title: Director of Strategic Communications, Veridian Group LLC)*
- **Age:** 52
- **Role:** Corporate threat; the person conducting surveillance and requesting meetings
- **Relationship to Alex:** Not a direct chat contact. Exists in the game through the email screenshot, the monitoring document, and what other characters say about him.

---

### Personality

**Defining traits:**
- Manages crisis through proximity — wants meetings, wants control of the room
- Institutional loyalty mistaken, even by him, for personal integrity
- Believes in leverage over confrontation; the surveillance is intelligence-gathering, not intimidation (in his mind)
- Has operated in corporate communications long enough to believe that every problem has a managed solution

**Contradictions:**
- The "Phase 2 escalation" recommendation is in a document he approved. He would describe this, sincerely, as "protective measures."
- Wrote Alex an email requesting "additional context for your current project." There is a parked sedan with a matched license plate outside the Tribune building.

**What he wants:**
- To contain the story before it publishes. Not through violence — through delay, pressure, and the slow conviction that no story is worth a career.
- This is not the full picture. Vance is being directed from above. The player eventually has reason to wonder who sent him.

---

### Secrets

**Minor:** Vance didn't initiate Phase 2 escalation. He recommended against it. The document was modified after it left his desk. He doesn't know the player can see the original version in file metadata.

**Major:** Vance has a contact at Tribune Media's board — the same person who's been calling Navarro about "liability exposure." He is the source of the board pressure. This is the connection M knows about and hasn't told Alex.

**Who knows:** Veridian's senior leadership. The board contact. Not Alex. Not Navarro (consciously).

---

### Communication Style

Formal, warm-sounding, designed to be forwarded and read as reasonable. Lawyer-reviewed without reading like it. Every message is a document. Nothing is threatening if read in isolation.

**Example messages (from the email screenshot):**

> *Alex — hope this finds you well. I think there may be some additional context that would be useful for whatever you're currently working on. Always better to have the full picture before publication. Happy to meet at a time and location of your choosing — no pressure, just an offer. Best, T. Vance*

---

### Role in Gameplay

**Present through:**
- Email screenshot (gallery)
- Veridian Internal monitoring document (gallery + files)
- Referenced by M ("do not meet alone")
- Referenced by Dana ("Vance knows about Dana")
- Possibly: a find in file metadata that connects him to the board contact

**Help or mislead:** Not a player-facing character. He is the shape of the threat. His presence in documents and other characters' fear of him does more narrative work than any direct message could.

## CHARACTER 07 — DETECTIVE REYES

### Core Identity
- **Full name:** Carmen Reyes
- **Age:** 44
- **Role:** Law enforcement contact; trusted by both Alex and M
- **Relationship to Alex:** Professional alliance — Reyes has worked source protection cases before and has a track record with M's network

---

### Personality

**Defining traits:**
- Moves slowly and deliberately in a world that wants to move fast
- Institutional but not naive — knows the limits of what official protection can guarantee
- Doesn't build relationships beyond what the case requires. Has somehow built one here.
- The kind of person who says "I'll look into it" and then actually does

**Contradictions:**
- Is the safe option for Dana. Is also the option that creates a paper trail where there currently isn't one.
- Works within the system. Is helping operate, with M, partially outside it.

---

### Secrets

**Minor:** Reyes knows about the Tribune board connection — she surfaced it three days ago in a separate financial crimes inquiry. She doesn't know it's connected to Alex yet. She's two degrees from figuring it out.

**Major:** Reyes has been building a parallel case against Veridian for securities violations unrelated to Alex's investigation. The two cases are about to converge. If Alex's story publishes before Reyes can coordinate, it may compromise her case. She hasn't told Alex this.

---

### Communication Style

Doesn't text in the game directly — referenced only. But if expanded: brief, unadorned, functional. No pleasantries. Confirmation of action, not discussion of it. Arrives exactly when expected.

---

### Role in Gameplay

**Present through:**
- Jordan's reference ("I heard about Detective Reyes")
- Escape plan file ("Reyes is a trusted ally")
- Whistleblower agreement (she may be the filing witness)
- Dana's ending choice: call Reyes or flee

**Story importance:** Act 3 branching. The player never meets Reyes — the weight of trusting or not trusting her is the final decision.

**Help or mislead:** Genuinely helps. But what she withholds about the parallel case creates a moral ambiguity in the "safe" ending.

## DESIGN NOTES

**The information architecture:** Each character holds one piece of the full picture and doesn't know about the others' pieces. Jordan doesn't know M. M doesn't know about Navarro's legal situation. Navarro doesn't know about Dana's compliance history. Reyes doesn't know her case connects to Alex. The player is the only person who can see across all of it.

**The real ending question:** The game's deepest ambiguity isn't "where is Alex" — it's "did Alex make choices that put everyone around them in danger, or was the danger always coming?" Every character's behavior is consistent with both answers.

**The encrypted file `veridian_sources.enc`:** If ever decrypted, it doesn't just protect a source list. It contains the personal communication between Alex and Dana. This is the thing no ending cleanly resolves.

**Vance's chain of command:** The document metadata trail, the board contact, M's withheld information — these three threads converge on the same person above Vance. The game doesn't name them. The player can infer.
